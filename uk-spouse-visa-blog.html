What is a UK Spouse Visa?

A UK spouse visa — formally the partner route under Appendix FM — grants permission to live in the United Kingdom alongside a British citizen or settled person. It covers spouses, civil partners, and unmarried partners who have cohabited for at least two years.

The relationship must be genuine, subsisting, and intended to continue in the UK. UKVI uses the same eligibility framework for all three categories.

Settlement Pathway

Initial visa (2 yr 9 mo outside UK) → Extension (2 yr 6 mo) → ILR after 5 years → British Citizenship.

Spouse vs Fiancé vs Unmarried Partner

All three routes fall under Appendix FM and lead to the same settlement pathway.

Who is Eligible?

Both the applicant and the sponsor must meet UKVI's requirements.

Qualifying Sponsors

Your UK partner must be a British/Irish citizen, hold ILR, EU Settled Status, or have refugee/humanitarian protection status.

Relationship Requirements

  • Met in person — no exceptions
  • Genuine and subsisting relationship at date of application
  • Intend to live together permanently in the UK
  • Any previous marriages ended permanently
  • Applicant must be 18 or over
Common Misconception

You cannot switch from a Standard Visitor Visa to a spouse visa while in the UK. This is one of the most frequent refusal reasons.

Financial Requirements 2026

The threshold for new applications is £29,000 gross (from 11 April 2024). Most failures happen because evidence is submitted in the wrong format, not because earnings fall short.

Which Threshold Applies?

SituationThreshold
New application after 11 April 2024£29,000/year
Extension (applied before April 2024, continuous leave)£18,600 transitional
Switched route after April 2024£29,000 — transitional lost
Critical Warning

Switching to the partner route after 11 April 2024 permanently removes access to the £18,600 threshold, regardless of relationship length.

Income Categories (Appendix FM-SE)

Category A — Salaried Employment

Six months of payslips, corresponding bank statements, and an employer letter on company letterhead confirming salary, employment type, and start date.

Category B — Self-Employment

SA302 tax calculations, tax year overview, business accounts, and six months of bank statements. UKVI uses net profit after tax, not turnover.

Category D — Cash Savings

Only amounts above £16,000 count. Must be held for six continuous months. Bank statements must be dated within 28 days of application.

Savings Formula

Required = £16,000 + (shortfall × 2.5). Savings-only with no income = £88,500.

How Caseworkers Assess Applications

  1. Consistency across all documents — a single date discrepancy raises doubt about the entire case.
  2. No gap-filling — a missing payslip is not assumed correct.
  3. No follow-up requests — they refuse what's submitted.
Key Warning

If a payslip is missing, a bank statement is outside the 28-day window, or an employer letter isn't on headed notepaper — the caseworker will refuse.

How to Apply: Step-by-Step

  1. TB test — chest X-ray certificate from approved clinic (valid 6 months).
  2. English language test — approved SELT provider from GOV.UK list.
  3. Police certificate — required from certain countries.

Country-Specific Notes

India

On the TB-testing list. Indian degrees taught in English can satisfy the language requirement via an ECCTIS letter.

Pakistan

Among the highest-volume countries. Interviews may be scheduled for couples who haven't cohabited. Marriage certificates must be from the Union Council (Nikah Nama with stamp) with certified English translation.

The Philippines

Divorce is not permitted for Filipino citizens. Previous marriages must have ended by annulment under Philippine law.
